## 2020 Trends

After a year of international travel that triggered my re-entry into bird watching, 2020 was a much more restrained year. While life events and global lockdowns kept me in the US, I enjoyed refining my birding skills and focusing on more birding from home.

Thanks to my new zoom lens, I was able work on my photography skills...

The other skill I worked to strengthen was birding by ear. While birding, the goal is species identification, not necessarily bird sighting. There many birds which are pretty good at staying out of sight but who make a lot of noise. Some species look so similar, like some species of crows, that they are practically impossible to accurately ID in the field without hearing their call.

While I experienced hundreds of new species as my wife and I traveled internationally, there were still a lot of "common" birds in the United States I had missed.... I also was able to expand my birding into some less common species groups, like Pelagic birds which spend most of their life on the open ocean.
